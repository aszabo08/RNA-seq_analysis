Comparing Htseq and STAR quantmode results with Salmon

   

In this experiment I explore other ways than Salmon to quantify the mapped reads at gene level to find out whether the choice of quantifying method has an impact on the results. In all cases STAR was used to map the sequence reads to the reference genome, DESeq for differential expression analysis and clusterProfiler for gene set enrichment analysis.

In conclusion the resulting count numbers are not identical, but highly similar in the three groups, where the highest counts were produced by Htseq and the lowest by STAR quantmode. The counts from Salmon and STAR quantmode are the most similar to each other based on the findings above which is supported by their lists of top ten enriched biological processes in the mutant samples which show 80% similarity. In addition, the number of differentially expressed genes in the mutant samples quantified by Salmon was 2194, by Htseq 2838 and by STAR quantmode 2251, showing similar results between Salmon and STAR quantmode.

The difference in the count numbers produced by the three different methods migth be explained by the different approaches of these software: Salmon uses “quasi-mapping” quantifying the reads at transcript level while STAR uses alignment-dependent mapping. With Htseq and STAR quantmode the genes were quantified at gene level and while in the event of a read mapping to multiple features the read was discarded with these software, it was counted proportionally with Salmon.

Based on the STAR manual: “With –quantMode GeneCounts option STAR will count number reads per gene while mapping. A read is counted if it overlaps (1nt or more) one and only one gene. Both ends of the pairedend read are checked for overlaps. The counts coincide with those produced by htseq-count with default parameters.” Alexander Dobin: STAR manual.pdf

The difference in count numbers between STAR quantmode and Htseq in this case might origin in the different settings of the two quantification methods.
